Chapter 12
The Salt Road

Nora Whitlock had taken the 8:14 from Halford Street for three years, and in all that time she had never once looked up.

This was not an accident. It was a system, and Nora believed in systems the way other people believed in horoscopes or their mothers. You boarded at the third door. You opened your book and you read, and you did not look up.

So she read. She was three sentences from the end of a chapter when the train did the thing the train was not supposed to do.
