# Cover Art Prompts — 13 NovelStack Originals

One prompt per book. Each is copy-paste ready for GPT image-gen
(or Midjourney / Imagen / Adobe Firefly — the language is generic enough).

**Append to EVERY prompt:**

> Vertical 2:3 book-cover composition. No text, no typography, no letters, no logos, no signatures anywhere in the image — the title will be composited separately. Leave a calm zone in the upper third where title typography will sit. Atmospheric, moody, not literal. Real-published-book feel, not stock photo. High contrast, cinematic.

When the image comes back, drop it into `assets/covers/<NN>_<slug>.png` and
run `scripts/cover_overlay.py` to composite the title typography on top
(font + position controlled by the catalogue entry).

## #06 The Housekeeper's Lie *(thriller · Hoover Verity)*

> A close-up photograph of a half-open mahogany bedside drawer in a dim Belgravia bedroom. Inside the drawer: a folded silk eye mask in dove grey, a lavender hand cream jar with a brass cap, and one face-down small black-and-white square photograph just visible at the edge. The drawer is lit only by lamplight spilling from off-frame at upper left, deep shadows pool inside. Dark teal-grey wallpaper visible behind the bedside table. Mood: domestic dread, quiet horror, something seen that shouldn't have been. Palette: charcoal, dove, brass, a single warm gold pool of lamplight.

## #07 Bound by the Don *(mafia romance · Penelope Douglas Credence)*

> A man's hand from the wrist down, resting on the leather inlay of a dark walnut desk in an unlit Brooklyn office at night, fingers slightly curled, signet ring on the smallest finger catching the only light in the frame from a brass desk lamp at off-frame upper right. A Montblanc fountain pen lies parallel to the top edge of an unseen contract beneath the hand. No face, no body — just the hand, the ring, the pen, the wood, the lamp light. Mood: dangerous, controlled, restrained menace, the calm before a decision. Palette: black, oxblood, deep brown, brass warm-white spotlight.

## #08 The Frostbound Court *(fantasy romance · SJM ACOTAR)*

> A long shot of a frozen river at dusk, a single mortal figure in a heavy dark fur-trimmed coat walking away from camera across the ice toward the opposite bank, where a pale-bone-coloured carved gate the height of a cathedral rises out of the mist. The gate is carved into the shape of two enormous interlocking hands holding the world. Snow drifts lightly, blue-grey twilight. No face visible. Mood: forbidden crossing, mortal courage in immortal terrain, fae fantasy with awe. Palette: ice blue, frost white, bone, deep mountain teal.

## #09 The Almost *(contemporary romance · Emily Henry Beach Read)*

> Two coffee mugs on a sunlit kitchen counter — one half-drunk black coffee, one untouched flat white with the foam still intact. A laptop open behind them with a Google Sheet visible but blurred. A sprig of rosemary lying between the mugs as if it has fallen out of someone's hand. Soft morning kitchen light, white-painted brick wall behind. The composition feels caught mid-moment, deliberately unstaged. Mood: warm, almost-romantic, the very faint hum of unfinished feeling. Palette: cream, sage green, terracotta warm tones, soft natural light.

## #10 The Hollow Hour *(YA dark academia · Olivie Blake Atlas Six)*

> A close-up photograph of a single cream-coloured letter resting on a green leather desk blotter at night, sealed with red wax stamped with the device of two crossed quills inside a circle of laurel. The wax has just been broken — split clean across the seal. Beside the letter, a black envelope with no return address. A green glass reading lamp at upper right casts the only light. Heavy walnut desk. Mood: ominous invitation, paranormal academia, a door that has been opened that cannot be closed. Palette: deep forest green, parchment cream, blood-red wax, brass lamplight.

## #11 The Daughter They Kept *(thriller · Verity Highland-set)*

> A weathered brass key on a faded green-painted door handle, the door slightly ajar, opening onto a darkened Highland-stone corridor inside an old grey-stone house. A single oil lamp visible on a side table down the corridor casting long shadows. The key is held by an unseen hand from the lower right edge of frame — only the fingertips touching the bow of the key visible. Mood: trespass, a door you were not given permission to open, isolation, Highland gothic. Palette: moss green, weathered brass, cold grey stone, single warm pool of lamplight far down the corridor.

## #17 The Brother of the Don *(dark romance · Penelope Douglas)*

> A second hand of a dark suit-cuffed arm holding a glass of espresso on a marble side table in an unlit Brooklyn parlour at night, signet ring on the smallest finger catching a single spotlight from off-frame. The ring sits noticeably higher on the finger than it would if the finger hadn't once been broken and reset wrong. Behind the hand, a leather portfolio on a desk just visible in shadow. No face. Mood: the brother nobody mentioned, restraint heavier than the original, a quieter danger. Palette: pavement grey, black wool, oxblood marble, brass spotlight.

## #25 The Crown of Hollow Years *(fantasy · Ava Reid)*

> A close-up of a single dark-green leather-bound book lying open on a stone window-sill of a high tower, the pages showing a hand-inked star chart in dark ink. A small silver pin shaped like a closed pin-on brooch rests on the open page. Beyond the window, a frozen lake at dawn in the distance, a pale flat winter light. A single white-bone bark fragment with iron-gall ink writing rests beside the book. Mood: a star-reader's quiet morning of dangerous reading, eight winters of waiting. Palette: dove grey, frost blue, parchment, silver, dark-ink black.

## #32 The Wedding Off-Season *(contemporary romance · Emily Henry People We Meet on Vacation)*

> A green leather-bound bookings ledger half-open on a wide wooden hotel reception desk, with a small black fountain pen lying across the open page. Beyond the desk, through a tall un-curtained window, a wet grey loch on Skye in winter under low overcast light, a single small fishing boat with a tiny yellow oil lamp at its prow visible at the end of a pier. A small framed black-and-white photograph of a young woman standing on hotel steps in 1981 sits on the desk corner. Mood: quiet hotel in the off-season, the past walking in through the front door. Palette: deep green leather, slate grey loch, single warm yellow lamp, cream stationery.

## #39 The Forwards *(sports romance · Hannah Grace Icebreaker)*

> Two ice-hockey team jerseys hanging on a single hook in a quiet locker-room — one navy with white lettering visible from behind (lettering itself blurred, no readable letters), one identical but slightly smaller, both with the same captain's C patch on the shoulder. The hook is brushed steel. Behind them, the polished pale wood of a locker-room bench. Cold blue overhead strip lighting from off-frame. Mood: two captains, rivals-to-lovers, shared rink, the moment before the moment. Palette: navy, white, steel, cold pale wood, blue-tinted light.

## #43 The Library at Vellichor *(YA dark academia · Bardugo Ninth House)*

> A close-up of a single fresh white camellia flower lying on the leather blotter of an antique librarian's desk under a green-shaded brass reading lamp at night. Beside the camellia, a folded pale-blue reader's slip and a black-bound notebook closed with a small red ribbon bookmark. The blotter is dark green leather worn at the corners. Tall narrow windows visible blurred in the background showing a night grounds. Mood: a librarian's first night, a flower nobody saw arrive, occult academia. Palette: forest green, blood-cream, brass, deep night blue at the edges.

## #48 The Other Girl in the Photograph *(literary drama · Liane Moriarty / Maggie O'Farrell)*

> A small walnut-framed black-and-white square photograph (held by an unseen hand from lower right by the corner only) of two young women — visible only from the shoulders down, both wearing the same plain summer cotton dress, both with the same loose left-shoulder braid, standing in front of a stone wall in 1960s sun. The photograph is held over the bare wood of a Welsh kitchen table. Beside the photograph on the table: a small green plate, a pair of reading glasses, a half-drunk cup of tea. Faces of the women in the photo are deliberately cropped above the chin — we see the dresses and the braids and the wall, not the faces. Mood: family secret carried for decades, the morning the photograph comes off the wall. Palette: sepia photograph tones, cream Welsh cotton, faded wood, soft natural window light.

## #52 The Beech Hill Reading Society *(cosy mystery · Richard Osman Thursday Murder Club)*

> A dark green wing-back leather armchair, empty, in a small upstairs reading-room of a village library at dawn. On the small oak side table beside the armchair: a single china teacup full of cold tea with the surface gone dust-skinned, a saucer with two untouched ginger biscuits, and a hardback novel open at a marked page with a small leaf-shaped paper bookmark in the gutter. A grey cardigan hangs over the back of the chair. Soft cold morning light from a window at frame right. Mood: a chair its occupant has left more than just temporarily, a cosy-mystery scene before the police arrive. Palette: deep forest green leather, cream china, oxford-blue cardigan, cold pale morning grey-light.

## Workflow

1. Copy a prompt above + the "Append to EVERY prompt" boilerplate
2. Paste into GPT image-gen (or chosen image model)
3. Save the result as `assets/covers/<NN>_<slug>.png` in the novelstack repo (e.g. `assets/covers/06_the_housekeepers_lie.png`)
4. Run `python3 scripts/cover_overlay.py <NN>` to composite the title typography (font + position read from `scripts/originals-catalogue.json`)
5. The composited cover gets uploaded to R2 by the loader (or by your existing cover-upload flow)
